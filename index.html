About Me

Chris was born in 1991 to two parents who, thanks to some very feminine ultra-sound pictures, were very were fully expecting to give birth to their third daughter. As a result, he spent two weeks without a name, before his parents settled on "Robert Clark." That was rejected by their 6-year-old-daughter, and it was changed again to Chris.


In 2013, he graduated with a degree in mechanical engineering from Rose-Hulman Institute of Technology. In 2019, after 5-1/2 years of working as a mechanical engineer, he decided that he desperately wanted to pursue a career that would never force him to use a hammer on anything ever again.

He has some rudimentary skills with python, and has been experimenting with creating an emulation of one of his favorite old boardgames using PyGame. He's got pathfinding and map drawing down, and is working on figuring out how to use sprites. Future project goals include making a few apps, including a means of playing drinking games with people in other states, so that users and their friends can continue to make poor life choices, even after college ends.

Future goals include designing some of the features for self-driving vehicles, and also making a fully functional Iron Man suit of armor, complete with the ability to create/transform into literally anything that the plot requires of it. One of these stretch goals seems far, far more likely than the other, especially if we're coding all of this in strictly HTML, CSS, and Java.
